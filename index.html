    Frequently Asked Questions

    • What does JCOP stands for?

      Joint Cybersecurity Operations Platform.

    • Who contributes to JCOP?

      Technical University of Crete (TUC), Greece - Project Coordinator
      Hellenic Ministry of Digital Governance - National Cyber ​​Security Authority (NCSA), Greece
      Sphynx Analytics Ltd (SANL), Cyprus
      Digital Security Authority of Cyprus (DSA), Cyprus
      Technische Universität Braunschweig (TUB), Germany
      University of Oslo (UiO), Norway
      Norwegian National Security Authority (NSM), Norway
    • Who co-finances JCOP?

      The European Health and Digital Executive Agency (HaDEA) under the 2020-2 CEF Telecom call.

    • What is the aim of JCOP project?

      JCOP will be a prototypical implementation of the Blueprint annexed in EC Recommendation C(2017)6100 on Coordinated Response to Large Scale Cybersecurity Incidents and Crises and a model that can be applied to all EU Member States.

      JCOP will facilitate capability for shared situational awareness, preparedness, and coordinated incident response with the cybersecurity structures and mechanisms established under the Directive on Network Security and Information Systems (NIS Directive).
